The Best 5 Anime Sports

shounen anime series tend to exaggerate for dramatic effect, and these 5 sports anime feature moments that are way over-the-top.

1.Blue Lock

Blue Lock is an anime originating from Japan, which tells about the struggles of a
group of soccer teenagers in achieving their dream of becoming the best striker in the world.


2.Haikyuu!

The story of Haikyuu anime is set around the lives of junior high school (junior high school) children in Japan.
It is said that there is a small boy named Shouyou Hinata who likes to play volleyball.
He also revived his school's volleyball club and joined the team of core players.


3.Yuri On Ice!!

Yuri on Ice tells the story of Yuri Katsuki, a skater who lost badly in the Grand Prix Final and other competitions. He took a break from his skating career and returned to his hometown. When he got home, Yuri copied the choreography of his idol skater from Russia, Viktor Nikiforov.

4.Kuroko No Basket

Kuroko reunites with the Generation of Miracles when former rivals are humiliated in an exhibition game against a notorious street basketball team.

5.Free!-iwatobi Swim club

Haruka Nanase has a love for water and a passion for swimming. In elementary school, he competed in and won a relay race with his three friends Rin Matsuoka, Nagisa Hazuki, and Makoto Tachibana. After claiming victory at the tournament, the four friends went their separate ways. Years later, they reunite as high school students; however, Rin couldn't care less about returning to the way things used to be. Not only does he attend a different school, but the sole thing important to him is proving that he is a better swimmer than Haruka.
